Profile of Dr. Mustapha Jibril

Dr. Mustapha Mohammed Jibril is a politician and public health physician born 44 years ago in Minna, Niger State. He had his primary and secondary education in Minna and then proceeded to the Ahmadu Bello University Zaria for his medical degree and subsequently Master’s in International Affairs and Diplomacy. He also holds a Master of Public Health degree from the University of Leeds, United Kingdom, in addition to various professional and leadership development courses, including from Harvard School of Public Health, Boston, United States of America. Dr. Jibril is a Fellow of the prestigious African Institute of Public Health Professionals.

Dr. Jibril was the Niger State Commissioner for Health from December 2015 to May 2019), responsible for providing technical and political leadership to the entire health sector. He implemented bold reforms to close longstanding gaps in health policy, infrastructure and human resources with a view to accelerating the attainment of universal health coverage (UHC) for the people of Niger State. Dr Jibril established the Niger State contributory health scheme backed by appropriate legislation and administrative structures; revamped the state drugs management agency to improve availability, affordability, quality and visibility of health commodities; increased health worker recruitment and deployment, improved pay package and engendered inter-professional harmony through deliberate policy measures; and introduced wholescale transformation of the primary health care system in the state. As a result, he attracted more than 5 billion naira in direct funding to the Niger State health sector from various external sources, including the Federal Government, Bill and Melinda Gates Foundation, World Bank etc. He was part of the second USAID/World Bank global UHC financing forum in April 2017 as the only Commissioner for Health from Nigeria and in May 2017 he participated at the first global expert convention on Learning for Action Across Health Systems in low-income countries.

Dr. Jibril was the founding Chair of the Nigeria Health Commissioners’ Forum and the forum’s representative on the National Steering Committee of the Basic Health Care Provision Fund (BHCPF-NSC). As the pioneer Chair, he played very prominent and pivotal role in the forum’s formation as well as in defining its structure, roles and responsibilities within Nigeria’s federated health system. The forum under his leadership recorded significant achievements in the area of improving policy cooperation and coordination between the Federal Government and the states as well as in fostering peer-to-peer exchange of best practice amongst State Ministries of Health. His role on the BHCPF-NSC included participating actively in providing cross-functional leadership, strategic operational direction, oversight, ensure programme visibility and serve as an advocacy group for increased resource mobilization. One key achievement in this area was the successful kick-off the BHCPF in 2019 after five years of repeated attempts.

In recognition of his excellent performance in the health sector, Dr. Mustapha Jibril was sent on a rescue mission to the Niger State Ministry of Investment, Commerce and Industry as the Commissioner with a mandate to fast-track diversification of the economic resource base of the state through the promotion of trade and investment, with emphasis on increased production of export commodities. He registered notable achievements aimed at stimulating local economic growth and diversification through industrialization, commerce and investment. His vision was to make Niger State a major investment hub in Nigeria and to leapfrog industrial development and wealth creation by optimizing capacity utilization in agro-allied, solid mineral and tourism sectors as well as developing community-based cooperatives to enhance local socioeconomic activities and self-empowerment. Specifically, Dr Jibril spearheaded the development of the Niger State Sector Specific Investment Incentive Policy which is the first of its kind in Nigeria, passage of the Bill for the Establishment of Niger State Investment Promotion Agency, 2), development of the Niger State Investors Guide to lead the way for investors into the state, review of fees and regulations on registration of business premises to improve ease of doing business in Niger State, issuance of the first Pioneer Status Incentives (3 years Tax Holiday) to KIARA Rice Mill Ltd in Pkasua of Mokwa LGA, upgrading of the NSDC’s accounting system from the old Statement of Accounting Standards (SAS) to International Financial Reporting Standard (IFRS).

He oversaw the completion of the first phase of Suleja International Market and Motor Parks comprising of modern shopping blocks, circulation and parking areas and organized open spaces, development of Garam Industrial Park comprising of 500 housing unit, including identification of seven investors; Minna Industrial Park with 90 units of SME factories by the developer, 25 industrial cluster units, five offices and ten units of Business Incubators, and identification of 810 hectares of land for ANFANI INDUSTRIAL PARK, including resettlement of original inhabitants and construction of access road and road network to and within the industrial park and resettlement area, upgrading of equipment at the shea processing facility in Kodo in Bosso LGA, completion of Commercial Office Complex in Minna which is projected to generate an annual income of ₦250 million income and boost commercial activities in the state, conceptualization and construction of Water Packaging Company and which upon completion will substantially increase the state’s revenue profile.

Dr Jibril’s also resuscitated six Microfinance Banks to boost economic and financial activities in the state, secured 10% share for Niger State Government from Hydroplolis Investment Limited, facilitated the participation of Niger State Government participation at Dubai Expo 2020 to showcase the state’s investment potentials, initiated Niger State’s inclusion in the State Export Expansion Program (SEEP) which will attract a ₦3billion facility from the Nigeria Export Import Bank, secured favourable judgment from Court of Appeal on the N574m investment fund recovery from ISSL, enhanced NSDC’s asset base through construction of Student Hostel at IBB University Lapai as well as Office Complex and Water Factory respectively. In the area of skills acquisition and people empowerment, he oversaw the training of 190 female entrepreneurs on Climate Smart Agriculture, shea value addition and shea factory operations in collaboration with UNWOMEN, IBB University, Lapai and the State Ministry of Women Affairs; branding and training of 40 entrepreneurs and seven cooperatives on cosmetics formulation and soap-making; training and certification of 50 young people on paint making, soap making, air freshener production; disbursement of ₦2 billion CBN MSME-DF loan to 12,033 beneficiaries in the State, successful implementation SMEs Clinic / Trade Money in Niger State in 2018/2019.

Prior to his appointment as the Commissioner for Health, Dr Jibril was the UNICEF State Coordinator for Niger where he led the design and rollout of the Hard-to-Reach (HTR) project, among other critical programmatic and leadership responsibilities. The HTR project was designed to reach hitherto underserviced populations with an integrated package of basic services as a strategy for interrupting Polio Virus transmission, increasing access to basic primary health care services to underserved populations and strengthening in-state capacity for planning and implementation of integrated outreach services to bridge inequity in health service delivery.

He worked previously as the Program Manager for the Global fund Health System Strengthening grant to the National Primary Healthcare Development Agency (NPHCDA), providing overall technical and programmatic leadership for the project team at the agency and in program states in collaboration with key partners like FMOH, NACA, SMOH, SPHCDA, WHO, USAID, DFID, among others. In this role, he coordinated the targeted distribution of Long-Lasting Insecticide Nets to about 6 million households as well as the training of more than 2500 frontline health workers on integrated PHC delivery, planned preventive maintenance of health infrastructure and equipment and integrated supportive supervision. He also served as the National Coordinator Nigeria’s National De-worming Program at the Federal Ministry of Health, where he transformed it from a mere desk office into a purpose driven public health program which successfully dewormed over 32 million children across the country. In the process, he gained vast experience in designing, implementing and monitoring public health programs as well as in managing the delicate relationships between states and the federal government and other critical stakeholders.

Between 2008 and 2011, Dr. Jibril was the Senior Special Assistant to the Governor of Niger State on International Relations and subsequently the Director General for Volunteer Service and Diaspora Relations to the Executive Governor of Niger State. During this period, he successfully pursued profound reforms in these hitherto uncharted waters, formulating a robust 4-year strategic plan to explore and harness the vast opportunities in volunteerism and diaspora relations. He is a founding member and active grassroots mobilizer for the governing All Progressives Congress (APC) in Niger State and has contributed immense time as well as mental and material resources to the growth and development of the party.

Dr Mustapha Jibril’s political ideology is borne out of an uncommon passion and commitment to positively impact the lives of his fellow constituents and the nation through policy and legislative advocacy and activism for systems reforms as well as collaborative action. His initiation into politics began in medical school during which he contested his first election for the position of President of the Ahmadu Bello University Medical Students Association. Subsequently, elected the Secretary General of the Nigerian Medical Students Association and later the Secretary General of the Federation of African Medical Students Association on account of his zeal and dedication to the promotion of the common good.

Since joining active politics in 2007, Dr. Jibril has aspired for the Chanchaga Federal Constituency position on two different occasions in the past, inspired by the zeal to mobilize and work with citizens at the grassroots in search of practical and enduring solutions to the multiple social and economic challenges facing Niger State and Nigeria. He has wholeheartedly devoted his time, talent and resources to politics and public service without let or hindrance. Through his singular devotion to the service of the people of Niger State, Dr Mustapha Mohammed Jibril has been able to grow have been able to attract reasonable political followership and grow the ALHERI brand within his immediate constituency and beyond.

Mission and Vision

  • To identify and prevent inefficiency and waste in government programmes and projects arising from extravagance, fraud, and misuse of public funds.
  • To determine the extent of government’s effectiveness in the exécution of public policy as determined by legislative enactments.
  • To ensure that MDAs, elected officials and government appointees are administering the law in a fair and proper manner throughout the states of the federation.
  • To gain knowledge and understanding of governmental programmes and processes and using same to improve law making and other legislative functions.
  • To use findings from legislative oversight to improve the legislative process and to hold the executive branch accountable for good governance, including invoking necessary disciplinary measures against elected and appointed officials found wanting in the discharge of their responsibilities.
